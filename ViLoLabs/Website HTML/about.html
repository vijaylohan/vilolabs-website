About ViLoLabs

Built to make
useful things.

An independent studio quietly building tools, learning resources, and data-driven knowledge for everyday people. We try to keep things simple, useful, and respectful of your time — and we're honest about how we evolve.

The simple idea behind all of this.

Most of the internet today is selling something. Even when it's free, it's mining your attention, your data, your habits. The trade is rarely fair.

ViLoLabs started from a much smaller idea — what if we just built things people actually need, and then got out of the way? A worksheet a parent can print in two clicks. A QR code that works without an account. A blog post that tells you the real petrol price across the border. A PDF tool that doesn't upload your file to a stranger's server.

That's it. That's the entire pitch.

What this means in practice.

For every blog post

  • Numbers verified against 2+ independent sources (regulators, official agencies, established outlets).
  • Twitter/X infographics, Reddit comments, and listicles are never accepted as primary sources.
  • Every source linked at the bottom. You can verify everything yourself.
  • Updates are timestamped. If data is older than 60 days for fast-moving prices (fuel, currency, gold), it gets refreshed or removed.

For every tool

  • Files don't leave your browser — everything processes locally on your device.
  • No mandatory signup to use the core feature.
  • If we add paid features later, the basic version stays free and useful — no bait-and-switch.
  • Visible reminder on each page that tools can make mistakes — always review output before printing or sharing.

For every worksheet

  • Free to print, free to share, free to use in classrooms.
  • Generation happens in your browser. We don't store what you generated unless you share or print it.
  • If you don't like the result, regenerate as many times as you want. We only count the ones you actually use.

How we keep the lights on.

Most sites hide this part. We'd rather just tell you. Our intent is simple — build things worth using, and earn enough from them to keep building for the long run.

Most of what we make is free, and stays free. Worksheets, tools, blog posts — open to everyone, no signup walls, no paywalls. To sustain that, we earn through two channels:

  • Affiliate links on blog posts and tool pages — only for products we'd actually use ourselves. Always clearly marked as affiliate, so you know.
  • Tasteful ads on some pages, picked to be relevant to what you're reading. No autoplay, no popups, no dark patterns, no fake urgency.

The lines we won't cross: your data won't be the product. We won't sell your information. We won't use deceptive flows. How we make money will always be visible on the page and easy to ignore if you want to. If something on the site ever feels pushy or off, tell us — we'll fix it.

On data — our commitment

Most of our tools run entirely in your browser, so your files never leave your device. If we ever build a tool that genuinely needs to send your data to a server (some things just can't be done client-side), we'll say so clearly on that tool's page — what's sent, why, where it goes, and how long it stays. No quiet uploads. No fine-print surprises.

What's next.

We're still small. Three product lines live, more in the queue. The roadmap is shaped by what people actually use — not by what looks impressive in a pitch deck.

Coming soon: a multi-language layer for worksheets so children can learn in their preferred language, more tools (PDF-to-Word, signature placement, watermark removal), and a simple newsletter for readers who want one good post a week instead of doom-scrolling for one.

No timelines. No countdowns. It ships when it's good.

Good to Know

Common questions.

How does UPI QR work?

You enter your UPI ID (like name@okhdfcbank) and an optional amount and note. Any UPI app (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM) can scan the QR and pay you directly. You can leave the amount blank to let the payer choose.

What error correction should I pick?

Medium is fine for most uses. Pick High if you plan to put a logo over the centre — High recovers up to 30% damage, so the QR still scans with the logo on top.

Are the colors safe?

Use dark dots on a light background. The bigger the contrast, the more reliable the scan. Inverted colors (light on dark) often fail on older phone cameras.

What if the QR doesn't scan?

Tick Print text below QR and the actual URL, UPI ID or WiFi network name gets printed right under the code — baked into the PNG/SVG you download, not just shown on screen. If a scan ever fails, whoever's holding it can just read and type the value in by hand. For WiFi, only the network name is printed — never the password, so it isn't left in plain sight next to the QR.

Can I generate many QR codes at once?

Yes — pick the Bulk tab, paste one URL or line of text per row (up to 100), and click Download all as ZIP. Every QR uses your chosen colors, error correction and margin. Each PNG in the ZIP is numbered and named after its source, so 100 lines still make sense in a folder.

Is any data sent to a server?

No — the QR is generated entirely in your browser. Even your UPI ID stays on your device.

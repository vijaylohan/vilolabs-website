Maths that grows with your child

A Class 3 child and a UKG child need very different sums, so the worksheet changes shape at every level. At LKG and UKG it's all pictures — count the apples, add two groups together, take a few away. By Class 1 the pictures are still there but the numbers climb. From Class 2 upward the pictures give way to number puzzles that make a child actually work the sum out rather than just count. Pick the class your child is in and you'll get maths pitched right for that age, not a one-size sheet.

There's no fixed pack of sheets to run out of. Every time you generate, the maker rolls a fresh set of sums. These free maths printables cover the core mathematics skills a child drills at school, so they can do a new maths worksheet every single day and never see the same page twice.

Why daily practice sheets help

Number sense is one of those things that comes down to repetition — the more addition and subtraction a child does, the faster and more confident they get, until the small sums become automatic and there's room in their head for the harder stuff. A few minutes of mental maths practice a day does more than a long session once a week. Because each sheet is free and prints on plain paper, keeping a child topped up with practice costs nothing but the ink.

Common questions

Which classes do these maths worksheets cover?

Seven levels: LKG and UKG, then Class 1 through Class 5. Each level is pitched at that age — LKG counts small groups of pictures, while Class 5 works with larger numbers and multiplication.

What kind of maths is on the worksheets?

Mostly addition and subtraction, growing in difficulty with each class. The younger levels use pictures to count and add; the older levels use number puzzles with bigger values. Multiplication appears from Class 5. There is no division or word-problem content yet.

Are the worksheets aligned to the CBSE or NCERT syllabus?

They cover the same foundational number skills a child practises at each class, and the difficulty is pitched to match, but they are general practice sheets rather than a chapter-by-chapter match to any one board's textbook.

Is it free, and can I print or save a PDF?

Completely free with no sign-up and no watermark. You can print directly or save the sheet as a PDF, and every click gives you a brand-new set of sums so you never run out of practice.
